    Common Banking Questions

    What's the difference between checking and savings accounts?

    Checking accounts are designed for frequent transactions and everyday spending, typically offering unlimited withdrawals, debit cards, and check-writing abilities but minimal interest. Savings accounts are meant for storing money and earning interest, with some limitations on withdrawals and typically no check-writing capabilities.

    How many bank accounts should I have?

    Most financial experts recommend having at least two accounts: a checking account for bills and daily expenses and a savings account for emergency funds and short-term goals. Some people benefit from multiple savings accounts dedicated to specific goals or a combination of accounts at different institutions to maximize features and benefits.

    Are online banks safe?

    Yes, reputable online banks are equally as safe as traditional banks if they're FDIC-insured (for banks) or NCUA-insured (for credit unions), which protects deposits up to $250,000 per depositor, per institution. Online banks typically use advanced encryption and security measures to protect your personal and financial information.

    What should I look for in a checking account?

    The best checking accounts have no monthly maintenance fees (or easy ways to waive them), no minimum balance requirements, extensive fee-free ATM networks, mobile and online banking features, and potentially interest earnings or rewards on debit card purchases.

    How do CDs work?

    Certificates of Deposit (CDs) are time deposits where you agree to leave your money in the account for a specific term (from 3 months to 5+ years) in exchange for a guaranteed interest rate. Typically, longer terms offer higher rates. If you withdraw funds before the CD matures, you'll usually face an early withdrawal penalty.
