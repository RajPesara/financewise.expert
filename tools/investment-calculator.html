Investment Strategy Tips

Start Early

Time is your most powerful ally in investing. Starting just 5 years earlier can increase your final balance by 30% or more due to the power of compound interest. Even small amounts invested early have time to grow substantially.

Stay Consistent

Regular, consistent contributions often outperform attempts to time the market. Dollar-cost averaging (investing a fixed amount at regular intervals) reduces the impact of market volatility and removes emotional decision-making.

Diversify Your Portfolio

Spreading investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) can reduce risk while maintaining returns. A well-diversified portfolio typically experiences less volatility while still capturing market growth.

Mind the Fees

Investment fees can significantly impact long-term returns. A difference of just 1% in annual fees can reduce your final balance by 20% or more over 30 years. Look for low-cost index funds and ETFs with expense ratios under 0.2%.
