Loan Tips & Strategies

Make Extra Payments

Adding just $100 extra to your monthly payment can save thousands in interest and shorten your loan term by years. On a $250,000 30-year loan at 5.25%, you could save over $35,000 in interest and pay off your loan nearly 5 years early.

Consider Refinancing

If interest rates drop by at least 0.5% below your current rate, it might be worth exploring refinancing options. Just make sure the savings on interest outweigh the closing costs and fees associated with refinancing.

Bi-weekly Payments

By switching from monthly to bi-weekly payments, you'll make 26 half-payments per year (equivalent to 13 monthly payments instead of 12). This simple change can save you thousands in interest and reduce your loan term by several years.

Points vs. Rate

When getting a new loan, evaluate whether paying points to lower your interest rate makes financial sense. Generally, if you plan to keep the loan for more than 5-7 years, paying points to reduce your rate can be beneficial in the long run.
